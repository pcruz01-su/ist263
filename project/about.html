About Us


Although Guatemala is a country rich in people, culture, and traditions, it is one of the poorest countries in Latin america. According to the World Food Program, 59% of people live in poverty with 23% of people living in extreme poverty. Guatemala has one of the highest levels of malnutrition in the world, this correlates with lack of hygiene, low levels of education, and lack of medical assistance. 42% of Guatemalans do not have access to drinking water, 45% of the population over 15 are illiterate, and most lack basic knowledge of hygiene that helps disease that are treatable in the developed world.

Our Story


In 2017 launched our #OneDropAtATime Campaign in an effort to help eradicate waterborne illnesses among the youth. By the end of 2018 we would have helped more than 400 families and over 2,500 kids with access to clean water through the distribution of water filtering systems.
